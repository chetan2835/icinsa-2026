Scope of Conference

Scope of Conference

The scope of ICINSA-2026 is to bring together researchers from mathematics, computing, and emerging interdisciplinary areas to share advancements in theory, applications, and data-driven technologies.

  • Algebraic Geometry and Combinatorics
  • Number Theory and Cryptography
  • Operator Theory and Functional Analysis
  • Special Function and Approximation Theory
  • Topology and Geometric Analysis
  • Group Theory and Representation Theory
  • Real and Complex Analysis
  • Mathematical Logic and Set Theory
  • Measure Theory and Integration
  • Fixed Point Theory and Applications
  • Mathematical Modelling and Differential Equations
  • Fractional Calculus and Its Applications
  • Optimization and Operational Research
  • Celestial Mechanics and Non-Linear Dynamics
  • Fluid Mechanics and Mathematical Biology
  • Continuum Mechanics and Material Modelling
  • Heat and Mass Transfer in Porous Media
  • Environmental and Geophysical Flows
  • Control Theory and Dynamical Systems
  • Mathematical Aspects of Climate and Sustainability Models
  • Probability and Statistics
  • Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, and Data Science
  • Statistical Inference and Regression Analysis
  • Stochastic Modelling and Simulation
  • Time Series Analysis and Forecasting
  • Bayesian Methods and Decision Theory
  • Big Data Analytics and Visualization
  • Mathematical Foundations of Deep Learning
  • Reliability Analysis and Risk Modelling
  • Bioinformatics and Statistical Genomics
  • Neural Networks and Image Processing
  • Soft Computing and Computational Intelligence
  • Fuzzy Logic and Rough Set Theory
  • Genetic Algorithms and Evolutionary Computation
  • Swarm Intelligence and Optimization
  • Expert Systems and Knowledge-Based Modelling
  • Natural Language Processing and Cognitive Computing
  • Reinforcement Learning and Intelligent Agents
  • Hybrid Intelligent Systems
  • Computational Vision and Pattern Recognition
  • Blockchain and Information Theory
  • Applied Mathematics in Engineering and Physical Sciences
  • High-Performance and Cloud Computing in Mathematics
  • Quantum Computing and Quantum Information
  • Mathematical Modelling in Biomedical Engineering
  • Internet of Things (IoT) and Smart Systems
  • Computational Finance and Econometrics
  • Cybersecurity and Encryption Algorithms
  • Mathematical Foundations of Network Science
  • Simulation and Modelling for Space, Energy, and Environment

Note: The conference scope encompasses a broad range of topics, and submissions are welcome on any aspect of mathematical sciences and computational intelligence, across various domains.
