Publication Policy: Special Issues & Proceedings (ICINSA-2026)

Q1. How are papers allocated to special issues?

Papers are allocated based on conference track and scope, updated on a rolling basis.

Q2. Which tracks are mapped to which journals?

  • Track 1 (Pure Mathematics): Special Issue of PJAA
  • Track 2 (Applied Mathematics): Special Issue of BSPM
  • Track 3 (Data Science & Statistics): Special Issue of RTA
  • Tracks 4 & 5: Conference Proceedings

Q3. Will all accepted papers be published in special issues?

No. A limited number will be selected; others may appear in proceedings.

Q4. What about papers from special sessions?

Papers related to Computational Intelligence will be published in proceedings.

Q5. Who makes the final decision?

The final decision lies with journal editors and the ICINSA-2026 Organizing Committee.

Full Paper Review Policy (ICINSA-2026)

All full papers submitted to ICINSA-2026 for publication in conference proceedings and associated journal special issues will undergo a rigorous peer-review process, following the principles and best practices outlined below.

1. Submission and Preliminary Checks

  • Initial editorial screening for scope and track relevance
  • Compliance with formatting and submission guidelines
  • Completeness of manuscript (abstract, keywords, references, figures)
  • Ethical standards, originality, authorship, and disclosures

2. Plagiarism and Ethical Compliance

  • All manuscripts are checked using plagiarism detection software
  • Papers with plagiarism, fabrication, or ethical issues will be rejected

3. Peer Review Model

  • Double-blind peer review process
  • Reviewers do not know author identities
  • At least two independent expert reviewers per paper

4. Review Criteria

  • Originality and novelty
  • Technical soundness and methodology
  • Relevance to conference theme and track
  • Clarity of presentation and conclusions
  • Adequacy of references

5. Review Outcomes

  • Accept without revision
  • Accept with minor revisions
  • Revise and resubmit (major revisions)
  • Reject

Authors must submit a revised manuscript along with a response-to-reviewers document within the stipulated time.

6. Final Editorial Decision

Revised papers are assessed by Track Editors / Editorial Committee. Final decisions regarding acceptance and allocation to proceedings or journal special issues are made in consultation with reviewers.

7. Allocation to Proceedings or Journals

  • Track 1 (Pure Mathematics): PJAA (Special Issue)
  • Track 2 (Applied Mathematics): BSPM (Special Issue)
  • Track 3 (Data Science & Statistics): RTA (Special Issue)
  • Tracks 4 & 5: Conference Proceedings

A limited number of papers may be selected for journal publication. Others will appear in conference proceedings based on quality and scope.

8. Author Responsibilities

  • All authors must approve the final manuscript
  • Proper acknowledgements and disclosures included
  • Revisions submitted within deadlines
  • Academic integrity maintained throughout

9. Transparency and Integrity

  • Fair, unbiased, and confidential peer review
  • Avoidance of conflicts of interest
  • Maintenance of academic credibility

ICINSA-2026 Publication Flow (Special Issues & Proceedings)

Step 1: Abstract Submission

Abstracts are received and screened for scope and conference track.

Step 2: Track Identification

The paper is assigned to one of the following tracks:

  • Track 1: Pure Mathematics
  • Track 2: Applied Mathematics
  • Track 3: Data Science, Statistics & Machine Learning
  • Track 4: Soft Computing & Computational Intelligence
  • Track 5: Emerging Applications & Interdisciplinary Computing

Step 3: Journal Mapping

  • Track 1 → PJAA (Special Issue)
  • Track 2 → BSPM (Special Issue)
  • Track 3 → RTA (Special Issue)
  • Tracks 4 & 5 → Conference Proceedings

Step 4: Editorial Evaluation

Papers are evaluated for quality, relevance, and journal requirements.

Step 5: Final Decision
